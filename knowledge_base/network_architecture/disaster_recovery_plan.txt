===========================
RECOVERY OBJECTIVES
===========================

RTO (Recovery Time Objective) for Tier 1 applications: 4 hours
RPO (Recovery Point Objective): 1 hour (maximum acceptable data loss)

===========================
APPLICATION TIERS
===========================

TIER 1 - Critical (RTO 4 hours, RPO 1 hour):
  - CBS (Core Banking System)
  - AUTH-SVC
  - PAYMENTS-GW

TIER 2 - Important (RTO 8 hours, RPO 4 hours):
  - FRAUD-ENGINE
  - REPORTING-SVC
  - SWIFT-ADAPTER

TIER 3 - Standard (RTO 24 hours, RPO 24 hours):
  - DATA-WAREHOUSE
  - Airflow Orchestration
  - Monitoring Tools (Zabbix)

===========================
DR SITE CONFIGURATION
===========================

Primary Site: DC-WEST (10.10.x.x subnet)
DR Site: DC-EAST (10.20.x.x subnet)

Tier 1 applications maintain hot-standby at DC-EAST.
Database replication: Oracle Data Guard (CBS), PostgreSQL streaming (AUTH-SVC, PAYMENTS-GW).
Replication lag must stay below 60 seconds.

DR DNS Failover: AWS Route 53, health checks, TTL 60 seconds.
Automatic failover for Tier 1 only.

===========================
FAILOVER STEPS
===========================

STEP 1: Platform Engineering Lead declares DR event.
STEP 2: AWS Route 53 DNS switch (automatic after 3 failed health checks, 10s interval).
STEP 3: Run validation script: /scripts/dr/validate_dr.sh from MGMT-NET jump server.
STEP 4: Notify all-staff@company.com within 30 minutes.
STEP 5: Monitor DR site for 2 hours before confirming stable.

Failback requires CAB approval. Minimum 24 hours stabilisation before failback.

===========================
DR TEST SCHEDULE
===========================

Full DR drill: Annually in Q4 (October)
Partial Tier 1 test: Q2 each year
Results reviewed by Risk Committee.
