Q: what is CBS?
A: Core Banking System. It's the mainframe-era system that holds all account data, balances, and 
   transaction history. When people say "the core", "the mainframe", or "account system" they mean CBS.
   Host: 10.10.1.10, Port: 8080. API endpoint: http://10.10.1.10:8080/api/v2/
   CBS is owned by the CBS team (cbs-ops@company.com). Platform eng does the infra.

Q: what's the difference between CBS and Payments Gateway?
A: CBS is the backend account ledger. Payments Gateway (PAYMENTS-GW) is the transaction processor.
   When a payment happens: Payments GW receives it → calls Fraud Engine for a risk score → 
   calls CBS to debit/credit the accounts → returns confirmation. 
   CBS doesn't talk to the outside world. Payments GW does. 
   PAYMENTS-GW host: 10.10.1.20, Port: 9000.

Q: why does fraud engine matter for payments?
A: Payments GW calls Fraud Engine (FRAUD-ENGINE) synchronously on every transaction.
   If fraud engine is down or slow, payments are blocked or delayed. 
   FRAUD_SCORE_THRESHOLD = 0.75. Above that score, the payment is held for manual review.
   FRAUD-ENGINE host: 10.10.1.30, Port: 8500.

Q: what is AUTH-SVC / why do I see 401 errors?
A: AUTH-SVC is the Keycloak SSO service. It's the identity provider for all internal apps.
   401 errors mean the auth token is invalid or expired. 
   If EVERYONE is getting 401s simultaneously: AUTH-SVC is probably down. Check health first.
   AUTH-SVC health: curl https://10.10.1.50:8443/auth/health (expected: {"status":"UP"})
   If just one user: account might be locked (AUTH_MAX_LOGIN_ATTEMPTS = 5 failed logins = locked).

Q: what is REPORTING-SVC and why is it sometimes slow?
A: Reporting Service (REPORTING-SVC) generates regulatory reports (LCR, NSFR, etc.)
   Host: 10.10.1.40, Port: 8200. It reads from Data Warehouse (Snowflake), not from CBS directly.
   When it's slow, check DWH first (Snowflake console), not REPORTING-SVC itself.
   The slowness in march 2025 was a missing index on fact_transactions (INC-2025-0288). 
   DBA added it. Should be fine now.

Q: what is Kafka used for?
A: Kafka is the message bus. Fraud transactions go through Kafka topic fraud.transaction.events.
   Kafka cluster is at 10.10.1.70-72 (3 brokers), port 9092.
   FRAUD_KAFKA_CONSUMER_GROUP = "fraud-scoring-cg-prod" - do not change this. 
   Changing the consumer group name caused INC-2024-0991 (offset reset, full reprocessing).
   Consumer lag on fraud.transaction.events is monitored by Zabbix.

Q: where is monitoring?
A: three places:
   - Zabbix: http://10.10.50.30 (infra, host health, disk, pool usage, kafka lag)
   - Datadog: https://app.datadoghq.com (APM, application metrics, dashboards)
   - Kibana: http://10.10.50.50:5601 (logs, from MGMT-NET only)
   - QRadar: 10.10.50.40 (SIEM, security events only)

Q: what are MGMT-NET and APP-NET?
A: VLAN segments. APP-NET (10.10.1.0/24) is where prod services run.
   MGMT-NET (10.10.50.0/24) is management: monitoring, bastion, ops tools.
   You SSH to prod via bastion host (10.10.50.5) - direct SSH to app servers is not allowed.
   All monitoring dashboards are on MGMT-NET.

Q: what is the release process?
A: All production changes go through CAB (Change Advisory Board).
   Emergency changes can be approved by platform lead + relevant team lead (as in INC-2024-1201 pool change).
   Release windows: every two weeks, alternating tuesdays. December is frozen (no releases dec 1-31).
   Release register is in release_change_register.txt.

Q: what is the december freeze?
A: After INC-2024-1201 (CBS pool exhaustion at december peak volume), the CTO approved a policy:
   no production releases December 1-31. The december period has 35% above normal transaction volume.
   Changes to cope with december (like CBS_DB_POOL_SIZE = 100) are done in October/November.
   December capacity review is now scheduled for October 2025.
